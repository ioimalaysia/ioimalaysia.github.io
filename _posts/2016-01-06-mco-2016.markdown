- 3 hours

[![google](http://ioimalaysia.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/03/google.png)](http://www.google.com/about/careers/locations/kuala-lumpur/)## Qualifying Rounds
3 qualifying rounds for the MCO will be held. In previous years, qualification was done through the [COCI](http://hsin.hr/coci/) and [USACO](http://www.usaco.org/) contests - we encourage you to continue doing those as practice.To those who attended the Training Camp in December, please note that some details have changed.- Qualifying rounds are open to everyone for participation. However, to be fair, only MCC 2015 participants can qualify for MCO 2016. Sign up at [http://bit.ly/mco-2016-quals-register](http://bit.ly/mco-2016-quals-register). It is very important that you fill this form up accurately.
- Your score will be the sum of your two best scores over the 3 rounds. We strongly recommend that you participate in all 3 rounds.
- The languages allowed are those of the official MCO 2016 contest (C, C++ and Java). In addition, for the first two qualifying rounds (Jan and Feb), Python will be allowed. We recommend that you transition to one of the official languages as soon as possible.
- The top 25-50 participants will be selected for the MCO 2016.
- Each round will have 6 problems, of similar difficulty to MCO 2016.
- Each round will last 6 hours. The problems should take only about 3 hours to solve. We are allowing 6 hours for the sake of flexibility.
- Each round will be held on a Saturday, from 4pm to 10pm (6 hours). The dates are 16 Jan, 20 Feb and 12 Mar.
- The contests will be held on [HackerRank](http://hackerrank.com/) - links to the contest will be sent out via email and posted on our [Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/ioimalaysia/).
